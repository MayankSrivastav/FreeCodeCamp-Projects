Dr. Dennis Ritchie

The man who defined modern computing

A quick view into Dennis Ritchie's life

  • 1941 Born in Bronxville, New York
  • 1961 Began working at the Bell Labs CSR Center
  • 1968 PHD thesis on Program Structure and Computational Complexity
  • 1969 Bell Labs Drops Multics Project
  • 1972 Invented C Programming Lanuage
  • 1973 Rewrote UNIX in C
  • 1983 Awarded A.M Turing Award for contributions to operating systems theory
  • 1988 Elected for U.S National Academy of Engineering
  • 1990 Appointed as the head of the System Software Research Department at Bell Labs
  • 1999 Awarded National Medal of Technology and Innovation
  • 2011 Dies at the age of 70

"Ritchie was under the radar. His name was not a household name at all, but... if you had a microscope and could look in a computer, you'd see his work everywhere inside."

computer historian Paul E. Ceruzzi
